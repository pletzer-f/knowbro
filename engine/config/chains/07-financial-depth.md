# Inference chain: Financial depth & indicative valuation

**Goal:** Go beyond revenue and margin — extract or derive every financially decision-relevant metric the data supports, and end with an indicative valuation range. A financial picture with only two numbers is not decision-grade.

**Derive each of these wherever the data allows (as estimates with full inference paths):**
- **Revenue per employee** vs the sector benchmark — and what the gap says (premium positioning, outsourcing, part-time distortion).
- **Absolute EBITDA** (from the revenue and margin chains) — state the most-likely value, not just the band.
- **Cash generation proxy:** EBITDA minus estimated maintenance capex (infer capex intensity from the fixed-asset base, depreciation hints, and sector norms) minus estimated tax. Express as "free cash flow before growth, most likely ~€X".
- **Working-capital posture:** receivables/inventory hints from the balance sheet, advance payments (machine builders, construction), factoring signals. Who funds the cycle — customers, suppliers, or the bank?
- **Liabilities decomposition:** when total liabilities exceed disclosed bank debt, decompose the remainder (trade creditors, advances, provisions, shareholder loans) using sector logic — never leave a liability block unexplained.
- **Debt capacity / headroom:** how much additional debt the company could plausibly service (e.g. 2.5–3.5× EBITDA minus existing net debt) — this is the acquirer's financing math.
- **Owner economics:** visible distributions, management compensation hints, related-party leases — what the owner actually takes out per year.

**Indicative valuation (always attempt):**
1. Take the EBITDA most-likely value and range from the margin chain.
2. Apply the sector/size multiple range from the exit chain (state it and its basis).
3. Adjust for the company-specific factors already established: owner dependence, customer concentration, asset backing, growth, capital intensity. State each adjustment's direction.
4. Output an EV range **with a most-likely value**, plus the equity-value bridge if net debt is estimable ("EV ~€18–24m, most likely ~€21m; less ~€1m net debt → equity ~€20m").
5. Confidence is capped by the weakest input — say which input caps it.

**What raises confidence:** filed P&L, capex history, depreciation schedule, facility terms, comparable transaction evidence in the niche.
