# Inference chain: Margin / profitability

**Goal:** An EBITDA-margin range and absolute EBITDA range consistent with the revenue estimate.

**Method:**
1. Start from sector EBITDA-margin comparables. State the comparable basis explicitly ("UK domiciliary care operators typically run 6–12% EBITDA", "alpine 4-star hotels 15–30% of revenue GOP, 10–20% EBITDA after rent/lease structure", "specialised machine builders 8–15%"). If listed comparables are relevant, name the kind of comparable used — but private small/mid-caps usually run below listed peers; adjust down and say so.
2. Adjust for company-specific signals:
   - **Size:** sub-scale companies usually sit at the low end of the sector band.
   - **Positioning:** premium/niche (custom engineering, specialist care, destination hotel) supports the upper half; commodity/price-taker the lower half. Infer positioning from product mix, customer types, pricing signals — and say which signals you used.
   - **Labour intensity & wage pressure:** sectors with regulated or scarce labour (care, hospitality, skilled trades) — check job-ad volume and offered wages as a margin-pressure signal.
   - **Balance-sheet clues:** equity ratio trend is a profitability echo — a rising equity ratio with no capital injection implies retained earnings, i.e. the company makes money. A shrinking one implies losses or distributions. Use it.
3. Cross-check the implied absolute EBITDA against any filed profit figure, tax disclosures, or dividend capacity signals.

**Output:** margin range + absolute EBITDA range derived from the revenue range (be explicit that errors compound: a low-confidence revenue estimate caps margin-derived EBITDA at low confidence too).

**What raises confidence:** filed P&L, payroll cost disclosure, equity-ratio time series, owner statements about profitability in press.
