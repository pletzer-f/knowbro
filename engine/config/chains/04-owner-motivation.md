# Inference chain: Owner motivation (PE-critical, almost never public)

**Goal:** Conclude what the owner probably wants — hold, sell, hand over, raise — and how that shapes a deal. This is the chain most analyses skip; do not skip it.

**Family / founder ownership:**
1. **Founder age & tenure:** estimate age from first registry appearance, education dates, or stated career history. Age 60+ with no visible successor = succession pressure building; 65+ = acute. State the age estimate and its basis.
2. **Succession signals:** family members appearing as directors/Prokuristen (positive for internal succession); external managing director hired alongside an aging founder (grooming for sale or professionalisation); founder removing themselves from operational roles; holding-company restructurings (often pre-sale tax structuring — a newly interposed Holding GmbH 1–3 years before is a strong pre-sale signal).
3. **Family vs institutional dynamics:** multiple family shareholders of a second/third generation = fragmented interests, often one branch wants liquidity → openness to minority buyouts or full sale. A single patriarch/matriarch = emotional attachment, price expectations above market, slow processes.
4. **Lifestyle/withdrawal signals:** real-estate-heavy balance sheets, persistent dividends, declining investment = harvesting mode. Heavy reinvestment = building mode, unlikely seller.

**PE / institutional ownership — fund vintage logic:**
1. Identify the owning fund and its vintage (closing year) from the acquisition date and any fund naming ("Fund III 2017").
2. A standard buyout fund has a ~10-year life with a ~5-year investment period. **A portfolio company held past year 5 of the fund's life is entering the exit window; past year 7–8 the fund is a near-forced seller** (LP pressure, fund extensions are costly). Compute: years held + fund age → exit pressure rating.
3. Cross-signals: bolt-on acquisitions stopped (harvest mode), CFO upgraded / "exit-ready" hires, vendor due diligence rumours, sale of sister portfolio companies, the sponsor raising a successor fund (needs DPI from this one).
4. Conclude: likely exit route (sponsor-to-sponsor, strategic, IPO unlikely below ~€250m EV) and the negotiating implication (a pressured seller trades price for certainty and speed).

**Output:** a plain conclusion — "the owner probably wants X within Y timeframe" — with the signals listed, the deal implication (price expectation, process dynamics, openness to structure like earn-outs / vendor loans / minority), and confidence. This will usually be low/medium confidence; that is fine. Be decisive anyway.

**What raises confidence:** direct statements in interviews, advisor mandates becoming visible, holding restructurings, fund lifecycle documents.
